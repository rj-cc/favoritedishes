Pork Sinigang

Description:

Sinigang is a sour soup native to the Philippines.

The quality of this dish depends on the souring agent. This is the ingredient that makes the soup sour.

The most common and widely used is unripe tamarind. It is known as sampaloc in the Philippines.

In 2021, travel guide TasteAtlas proclaimed our humble dish to be the best vegetable soup in the world!

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs pork belly
  • 1 bunch kang-kong leaves
  • 3 tbsp fish sauce
  • 12 pieces string beans
  • 2 pieces tomato quartered
  • 3 pieces green chili
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil
  • 2 quarts water
  • 1 piece onion sliced
  • 2 pieces taro quartered
  • 1 pack sinigang mix

Steps:

  1. Heat the pot and put-in the cooking oil.
  2. Saut√© the onion until its layers separate from each other.
  3. Add the pork belly and cook until outer part turns light brown.
  4. Put-in the fish sauce and mix with the ingredients.
  5. Pour the water and bring to a boil.
  6. Add the taro and tomatoes then simmer for 40 minutes or until pork is tender.
  7. Put-in the sinigang mix and chili.
  8. Add the string beans (and other vegetables if there are any) and simmer for 5 to 8 minutes.
  9. Put-in the kang-kong, turn off the heat, and cover the pot. Let the it cook using the remaining heat in the pot.
  10. Serve hot. Share and enjoy!
